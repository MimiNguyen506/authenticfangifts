Frequently Asked Questions

Is Scooby-Doo merchandise officially licensed?

Yes — Scooby-Doo is owned by Warner Bros. Discovery, which operates an active licensing program. Products sold through SenPrints carry official Warner Bros. licensing agreements. You can verify by checking for the official licensed hang tag on arrival. If there's no tag or the tag looks generic, it's almost certainly a counterfeit.

How much does officially licensed Scooby-Doo merch cost?

Officially licensed Scooby-Doo t-shirts typically range from $19–$28 USD. Hoodies and sweatshirts run $38–$50. If you see Scooby-Doo apparel priced under $12 on a marketplace, it is almost certainly an unlicensed bootleg — licensed manufacturing has royalty costs and quality minimums that push prices above that threshold.

Who owns the Scooby-Doo license?

Scooby-Doo is owned by Warner Bros. Discovery (the merged entity of WarnerMedia and Discovery). The character was originally created in 1969 by Hanna-Barbera, which was later acquired by Turner Broadcasting and then Warner Bros. WB manages all licensing partnerships — including their agreement with SenPrints. Purchasing from unlicensed sellers funds counterfeiters instead of the creators and rights holders.
