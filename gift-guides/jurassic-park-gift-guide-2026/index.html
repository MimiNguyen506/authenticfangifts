The Problem With "Jurassic Park Gifts" on Amazon and Etsy

Search "Jurassic Park shirt" on any major marketplace and you'll get thousands of results in seconds. That's the problem. The instantly recognizable red T-Rex skeleton logo and that specific cracked-egg typeface are among the most bootlegged movie assets on the internet — right up there with band tees and vintage sports logos. Most of what you're scrolling through was never approved by Universal, never paid a licensing fee, and was printed by someone who grabbed a low-res screenshot off Google Images.

It gets worse once the package actually arrives. The "InGen" font is slightly off. The T-Rex silhouette looks stretched or pixelated up close. The fabric is a thin blend that pills after two washes. And because these sellers operate outside any licensing agreement, there's no accountability if the print cracks a week later — no brand standard they were ever held to in the first place. That's a rough way to find out your "cool dino shirt" was a $9.99 knockoff all along.
