The Problem With "Powerpuff Girls Merch" on Etsy and Redbubble

Search "Powerpuff Girls gift" right now and nearly every result sends you to the same three places: Etsy, Redbubble, TeePublic. Cute designs, sure — but almost none of it is officially licensed. It's independent print-on-demand artists reproducing Blossom, Bubbles, and Buttercup without a licensing agreement with Cartoon Network or Warner Bros. Discovery.

That matters more than it sounds. Unlicensed listings get pulled without warning when the platform's IP enforcement catches up, which means an order can be cancelled or a shop can vanish between "add to cart" and "delivered." The print quality is a coin flip — some shops screen print well, plenty just heat-press a low-res image that cracks after two washes. And because there's no accountability to the actual rights holder, sizing, fabric weight, and color accuracy vary wildly from seller to seller. Buy three "identical" Powerpuff tees from three different Etsy shops and you might get three different products.
